Division Rules & Classes

What are the weight classes for NPC Men's Bodybuilding?

NPC Men's Bodybuilding features 6 weight classes plus Open division: Bantamweight (up to 143.25 lbs), Lightweight (over 143.25 up to 154 lbs), Middleweight (over 154.25 up to 176 lbs), Light Heavyweight (over 176.25 up to 198 lbs), Heavyweight (over 198.25 up to 225 lbs), Super Heavyweight (over 225 lbs), and Open (all weights compete together). At MetroFlex Events, weigh-ins occur the morning of prejudging at the venue.

How does the NPC Classic Physique division work and what are the height/weight requirements?

Classic Physique is a height-based division emphasizing the aesthetics of Golden Era bodybuilding. Classes include: Up to 5'4" (167 lbs max), Over 5'4" to 5'5" (172 lbs max), Over 5'5" to 5'6" (177 lbs max), Over 5'6" to 5'7" (182 lbs max), and so on with incremental increases. You'll be measured for height and weighed at check-in. Competitors wear board shorts (above the knee, below mid-thigh) and perform mandatory poses plus a 60-second posing routine to music at finals.

What are the height classes for NPC Men's Physique?

Men's Physique is divided into 8 height classes: Class A (up to 5'6"), Class B (over 5'6" to 5'7.5"), Class C (over 5'7.5" to 5'8.5"), Class D (over 5'8.5" to 5'9.5"), Class E (over 5'9.5" to 5'10.5"), Class F (over 5'10.5" to 5'11.5"), Class G (over 5'11.5" to 6'1"), and Class H (over 6'1"). Competitors wear board shorts and are judged on overall athletic physique, V-taper, and stage presence. No mandatory poses - judges use quarter turns and call-outs.

What's the difference between NPC Bikini, Wellness, Figure, Women's Physique, and Women's Bodybuilding divisions?

Bikini emphasizes feminine curves with minimal muscle definition, full round glutes, and toned presentation. Wellness features athletic physiques with more mass in hips, glutes, and thighs compared to upper body - think athletic lower body development with bikini-style conditioning. Figure adds more overall muscle tone and definition than Bikini with symmetrical development. Women's Physique shows visible muscle separation and striations with 8 mandatory poses plus routine to music. Women's Bodybuilding is the most muscular division with extreme development, full posing routine, and weight-class competition. Each division has specific posing and presentation requirements.

Can I compete in multiple divisions at the same show (crossovers)?

Yes, most MetroFlex Events allow crossovers between divisions at the same show. Popular crossovers include: Bikini and Wellness, Figure and Women's Physique, Men's Bodybuilding and Classic Physique. Each division is a separate entry with its own fee. You'll compete in prejudging for each division and potentially finals for each if you place well. Consider your conditioning carefully - some divisions require different levels of muscularity and conditioning. Crossovers are a great way to find which division suits your physique best, especially for first-time competitors.

What are Novice, Open, Masters, and Teen/Collegiate classes?

NOVICE: For competitors who have never placed 1st in their class at any NPC competition. Great for first-timers to compete against other newcomers. OPEN: Open to all competitors regardless of experience - where the best of the best compete. MASTERS: Age-based divisions (typically 35+, 40+, 45+, 50+, 55+, 60+) for competitors over specific ages. TEEN: For competitors aged 13-19. COLLEGIATE: For current college students with valid student ID. You can compete in multiple classes (e.g., Novice Middleweight AND Open Middleweight) at the same show - each is a separate entry. This is common strategy for new competitors to maximize stage time.

Attire & Presentation

What are the suit requirements for each NPC division?

Men's Bodybuilding: Standard posing trunks with at least half rear coverage (no pulling up sides during poses). Classic Physique: Board shorts above knee, below mid-thigh. Men's Physique: Board shorts (specific brand/style approved by NPC). Women's Bodybuilding/Physique/Figure: Two-piece suit with V-shaped bottom, at least half rear coverage - thongs prohibited. Bikini/Wellness: Two-piece bikini with V-shaped bottom, minimum half rear coverage, no thongs. Important: Bring TWO suits to check-in in case judges deem one improper. MetroFlex Events recommends checking suit compliance before competition day.

What tanning products are allowed and prohibited at NPC competitions?

ALLOWED: Tanning products that produce a natural tone mimicking a real tan. Apply evenly for a professional appearance. PROHIBITED: Dream Tan is absolutely banned at all MetroFlex Events. Products creating unnatural tones (orange, yellow, red, green, gold hues) are not allowed. Bronzing agents producing metallic looks are prohibited. Excessive oils, moisturizers, or creams are not permitted - use only in moderation. Tanning must be completed before arriving at the venue. No airbrush tanning equipment or tanning backstage is allowed. Violators and their competitors will be disqualified.

What can't I wear or bring on stage during competition?

PROHIBITED ON STAGE: Footwear (except women's heels in appropriate divisions), watches, bangles, pendants, chains, bracelets (wrist or ankle), excessive ornamentation, artificial aids to physique, props of any kind. JEWELRY: Most divisions allow minimal stage jewelry (small earrings, simple necklace for women) but check specific division rules. Some divisions prohibit all jewelry. EXCESSIVE OIL: Moderate application only - excessive oils, moisturizers, or creams are prohibited. Judges can ask you to remove excess oil. At MetroFlex Events, we maintain strict standards to ensure fair competition. Check your division's specific rules and when in doubt, keep it simple and classic.

Competition Procedures

How long can my posing routine be and when do I need to submit music?

Posing routines for Men's & Women's Bodybuilding, Classic Physique, and Women's Physique are maximum 60 seconds. Music must be uploaded to your Muscleware account at least 5 days before the event. Online upload closes when online entries close. If you miss the deadline, bring music on USB drive to check-in (have a backup copy). Your music file should contain ONLY your 60-second song, properly edited. At MetroFlex Events, only top finalists may perform routines depending on competitor numbers - the head judge and promoter decide.

When and where is weigh-in and registration check-in?

Check-in and weigh-ins occur the day of the competition, typically the morning of prejudging at the venue. ALL competitors regardless of division must attend official check-in. You'll register, present your NPC membership card, receive your competitor number, and (if required for your division) be weighed and height-measured. For weight-class divisions (Bodybuilding, Classic Physique), if you don't make weight, you have a maximum 30 minutes to return and re-weigh. Failure to make weight within 30 minutes results in elimination. Arrive early - check-in can take time with large competitor fields at MetroFlex Championship Series events.

How does the judging process work at NPC competitions?

NPC shows have two main sessions: PREJUDGING (morning): This is where placements are decided. Judges score you during comparisons and call-outs. Competitors perform mandatory poses or quarter turns depending on division. Judges take detailed notes and rank competitors. Your prejudging performance determines 100% of your placement. FINALS (evening): Entertainment show for audience. Awards are presented. Some divisions perform posing routines. Finalists are announced (typically top 5-6 per class). Finals don't change your placing - it's decided at prejudging. Both sessions require separate tickets for spectators at MetroFlex Events. Competitors receive complimentary finals admission.

What is the typical competition day timeline?

TYPICAL METROFLEX EVENTS SCHEDULE: 7:00 AM - 9:00 AM: Athlete check-in, weigh-ins, height measurement (arrive early). 9:00 AM - 9:30 AM: Competitor meeting and stage walkthrough. 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM: PREJUDGING - All divisions compete, judging and comparisons (this is where placement is decided). 2:00 PM - 5:00 PM: Break time (competitors rest, eat, prepare for finals). 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Doors open for finals, audience arrives. 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM: FINALS/EVENING SHOW - Top finalists presented, posing routines, awards ceremony, overall winners announced. Times vary by show and number of competitors. Big shows like the Ronnie Coleman Classic 30th Anniversary may run longer. Check your specific event schedule on MetroFlexEvents.com closer to show date.

How do I earn my IFBB Pro Card at a MetroFlex Events show?

Great for first-time competitors. No pro cards awarded. NATIONAL QUALIFIER SHOWS (like MetroFlex Events): Require national qualification (top 2 at regional in Open class). Judging Criteria

What are judges looking for in each division?

MEN'S BODYBUILDING: Total package - balance of size, symmetry, and muscularity. CLASSIC PHYSIQUE: Aesthetics, V-taper, Golden Era proportions with height/weight limits. MEN'S PHYSIQUE: Fit, athletic look with shape, symmetry, and muscularity - extreme mass is marked down. BIKINI: Feminine shape, full round glutes with slight hamstring separation, toned core, small shoulder roundness - no muscle density or striations. WELLNESS: Athletic physique with more lower body mass (hips, glutes, thighs) than upper body, slight separation but no striations. FIGURE: Balanced muscle tone and definition, symmetry, no overpowering body parts. WOMEN'S PHYSIQUE: Figure aesthetics with more muscularity, visible separation, some striations, 8-10% body fat. WOMEN'S BODYBUILDING: Extreme muscle development, separation, conditioning, and symmetry.

How are ties broken if two competitors have the same score?

If competitors tie in overall scoring, NPC rules use a tiebreaker system based on individual judge scores. The head judge reviews all scorecards to determine which competitor received more higher placements from individual judges. If still tied, the head judge makes the final decision based on their assessment of overall presentation, symmetry, conditioning, and stage presence. In practical terms at MetroFlex Events with experienced judging panels, ties are rare because judges use detailed scoring criteria. Every detail matters - conditioning, symmetry, presentation, and confidence can all influence close decisions. This is why bringing your absolute best package matters.

First-Time Competitors

What should I know as a first-time NPC competitor?

1) START EARLY: Choose a show 12-24 weeks out depending on how much body fat you need to lose. 2) PICK YOUR DIVISION: Don't stress - try one and learn. Crossovers let you test multiple divisions. 3) HIRE A COACH: Diet and posing coaches are game-changers for first-timers. 4) ATTEND A SHOW: Watch a MetroFlex Events competition first to see how it works. 5) PRACTICE POSING: Equally important as training. Practice daily. 6) JOIN NOVICE CLASS: Compete against other newcomers plus Open class for experience. 7) GET NPC CARD EARLY: Sign up at NPCOnline.com before registering for shows. 8) CHECK SUIT COMPLIANCE: Bring two suits to check-in. 9) MANAGE EXPECTATIONS: It's a learning experience. Have fun and enjoy the process. 10) PLAN POST-SHOW NUTRITION: Don't binge - reverse diet properly. MetroFlex Events welcomes first-timers and celebrates their courage!
